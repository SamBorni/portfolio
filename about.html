About

Sam Borni

Sam Borni is a freelance writer, a Double Masters student at King's College London and Renmin University of China, and a Senior Solutions Consultant at TIBCO Software. He is an expert at mastering data to enable businesses in optimising their strategic and real-time decision-making processes. Adept working within a number of life-cycle models, and well-versed in a number of programming languages including Python, SQL, and Java, Sam has seven years of experience leading enablement sessions to equip customers with the necessary skillsets to establish robust master data management initiatives, data governance frameworks and software asset management practices.

Sam also has extensive experience working across Europe and Asia, and is passionate about international politics and diplomacy. A strong proponent of international organisations and a staunch advocate for multilateralism, he is currently researching the possibility of a renewed Anglo-Japanese alliance in the aftermath of Brexit. Sam graduated with honours from the University of Portsmouth in 2015 with a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, and has a fundamental understanding of Japanese language.

An avid athlete, Sam has won awards playing both rugby and American football, and regularly competes in weightlifting competitions. He is also a former award-winning writer for The Galleon newspaper, and you’ll find him online @samborni on social media, or in-person at London coffee shops that make a good flat white.
